CANADA HEARTBREAK & HEROICS: Spiffy Wins Again as Davensport Crashes Out on Lap 1!

Disaster strikes the championship leader as he DNFs early, while teammate Spiffy capitalizes to extend his title lead. Red Bull 2-3 limits Ferrari's joy.

MONTREAL, CANADA – The treacherous Circuit Gilles Villeneuve delivered its signature blend of triumph and tragedy, leaving the Ferrari garage with wildly mixed emotions. **Spiffy (#77)** continued his sensational run of form, converting a P4 start into his **fifth victory** of the season with another cool, calculated drive.

But while Spiffy celebrated, his teammate and now *former* championship leader **Davensport (#15)** suffered a catastrophic start to his race. Starting P10, the aggressive American's race lasted less than one lap. After an initial warning for contact with Liam Lawson, Davensport dramatically crashed into the wall exiting the final corner – the infamous "Wall of Champions" – ending his day immediately with terminal damage.

It's the second consecutive disastrous weekend for Davensport, following his P20 qualifying in Imola. While his recovery drive there salvaged points, this Lap 1 DNF is a massive blow to his championship campaign, marking his first non-score of the season.

"Unacceptable. Just unacceptable. I pushed too hard, got too greedy on exit, and the wall punished me. No excuses. I let the team down, and I gave Max and Spiffy a huge gift."
- Davensport

Championship Ramifications

Spiffy's win, coupled with Davensport's DNF, significantly shifts the title battle. Spiffy (158 pts) now holds a commanding **26-point lead** over Verstappen (132 pts) who jumps to P2. Davensport (125 pts) drops to third. **Max Verstappen** finished P2, with teammate **Yuki Tsunoda** completing the podium in P3, securing a vital double-podium for Red Bull.

Spiffy's consistency ("Ice") is proving incredibly effective, while Davensport's high-risk approach ("Fire") has now cost him dearly two weekends in a row. Ferrari still comfortably leads the Constructors', but the internal dynamic between their two star rookies has taken another dramatic turn heading into the European heart of the season.
