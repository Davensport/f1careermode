DAVENSPORT DOES THE DOUBLE! Dominates Belgian GP from P2 After Sprint Win

"Fire" is back! Davensport takes commanding victory, Spiffy recovers to P4 after collisions, Verstappen falters from pole to finish P3.

SPA-FRANCORCHAMPS, BELGIUM – **Davensport (#15)** emphatically silenced any doubts about his championship credentials with a stunning lights-to-flag victory at the Belgian Grand Prix. Following up his Sprint win, the Ferrari rookie started P2 but seized the lead early and never looked back, delivering a dominant performance reminiscent of his early-season form.

It marks a spectacular turnaround for Davensport after a DNF in Canada and qualifying last in Imola. This "Spa Double" – winning both the Sprint and the main GP – nets him a massive 33 points (25 + 8) for the weekend, reigniting his title challenge.

His teammate, **Spiffy (#77)**, endured a much scrappier race. Starting P5, the championship leader was involved in separate collisions with Fernando Alonso and Oliver Bearman, earning warnings for both incidents. Despite the contact, the "Smooth Operator" salvaged a crucial **P4**, limiting the points loss to his resurgent teammate.

"This weekend was exactly what I needed. Sprint win, GP win... the car felt incredible. We showed our pace when things go right. The championship fight is far from over."
- Davensport

Verstappen Fails to Convert Pole, Championship Battle Intensifies

The biggest loser of the day was arguably **Max Verstappen**. Starting from Pole Position, the Red Bull driver couldn't match Davensport's pace and ultimately finished **P3**, overtaken by Mercedes' impressive **George Russell** who claimed P2 after starting P4. Verstappen losing out on the win from pole is a significant missed opportunity.

Spiffy (200 pts) still leads the championship, but Davensport's perfect weekend (162 pts) slashes the gap down to **38 points**. Verstappen (153 pts) remains third but falls further behind Spiffy. Ferrari (362 pts) extends its already huge Constructors' lead over Red Bull (217 pts) and McLaren (210 pts).

The "Fire and Ice" battle is back on boil as the season heads towards its final stretch.
